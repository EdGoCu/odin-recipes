Classic Donuts

Description

A donut can be plain or glaze with any type of topping we desire.It is up to us how far can we go.In this ocasion we will leave everything in the hands of our beautiful and messy pantry and add anything we see and like.

For my case in particular,I'll just add a simple glaze,made of powdered sugar and a splash of milk (water is fine too,althought richness of the mixture will vary).Oh! and also some coconut powder,leftover ingredient of a coconut macaron (recipe coming soon..).

Ingredients

  • 850 gr. All purpose flour
  • 14 gr. Kosher Salt
  • 56 gr. Honey
  • 7 gr. Packet Active Dry Yeast
  • 110 gr. Unsalted butter
    • Mixed all the ingredients up to this point in a bowl or stand mixer
  • 4 medium/big size eggs
  • 25 gr. Honey
  • 1 Tbs vanilla Extract
  • 450 gr. Whole Milk

Steps

  1. Whisk well the last set of ingredients in separate bowl.
  2. Slowly add mixture to our bowl of dry & fatty ingredients.
  3. Mix until our dought is well combined,feels smooth in our hands and has achieve a windowpane test.A reference of the texture we desire,by King's Flour Official website Brioche Bread.
  4. Let it sit for about an hour,or has doubled in size,as soon after place it in a refrigerator and let it chill until cold.
  5. Place the dough onto a floured surface (not Tony Montana's desk amount) and roll out until 13 mm. reached.
  6. Cut doughtnuts as you please (keep it classic like me!) and arrange them in a piece of paper to proof.
  7. It can take 30 minutes to 10 minutes,so when ready drop them in oil at 165 Celcius until cooked or golden brown.
  8. Let them cool on a wire rack if possible
  9. And here it is, if kids presents, when the fun part begins.Time to top them off with whatever we want.I'll keep it simple as said before.
